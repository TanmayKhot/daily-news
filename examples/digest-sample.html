[HN] CNNs + VLM outperforms pure VLMs on OCR

3 points · 0 comments · 7h ago

Article

  • Pure CNNs for OCR deliver pixel-perfect bounding boxes and per-character confidence scores but produce unstructured output with no semantic understanding; pure VLMs provide flexible structured schemas and semantic reasoning but generate imprecise bounding boxes and hallucinate on low-quality inputs.
  • Generic vision encoders in VLMs (like SigLIP 2) are trained on broad image-text alignment rather than text recognition; they lack the high spatial resolution and stroke-level detail needed for dense, rotated, or noisy text.
  • Tool-calling approaches that invoke a CNN OCR specialist improve accuracy but add latency and fail when the specialist output must appear byte-for-byte in the final response with cross-checking between models.
  • Interfaze's architecture fuses a dedicated OCR CNN directly into the VLM decoder alongside a generic vision encoder, allowing the CNN to provide character-level tokens and grounded bounding boxes while the LLM decoder handles reasoning and structured output in a single call.
  • The combined approach returns structured JSON with semantic understanding and flexible schemas while preserving CNN-generated bounding box metadata and confidence scores in the same response without additional tool-call overhead.
